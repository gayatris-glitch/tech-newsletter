When Claude Forgot Everything

Featuring Riya Menon | Senior Full-Stack Dev @ TestMu AI

01. The Setup

So picture this: You’re mid-conversation with Claude Code, vibing, getting actual useful test automation suggestions. Then you ask a follow-up question and… blank stare energy. Claude acts like you two just met.

"Customers were NOT having it. Neither was Riya."

02. The Problem

Multi-turn conversations were breaking. Not dramatically—just… awkwardly. Claude would nail the first few exchanges, then randomly lose the plot. Like that friend who forgets what you said 30 seconds ago.

“It wasn’t a crash. It wasn’t an error. It was just… wrong answers,” Riya explains. “The worst kind of bug—the kind that gaslights you.”

03. The Rabbit Hole 🐇

Riya’s first instinct? Token limits. She dug through context windows, checked payload sizes. Fine.

Next theory: Race conditions. Maybe async calls were stepping on each other? Clean.

“I spent two days convinced it was a caching issue. Spoiler: it wasn’t.”

04. The Aha! Moment 💡

Day three. 2 AM. Riya notices something weird. The context was being passed correctly… but the conversation history was being serialized in the wrong order. Message 3 before Message 1.

> "The moment I saw it, I literally said ‘no way’ out loud. It was so dumb. So obvious. But also completely invisible until you REALLY looked."
✔ The Fix: A single sorting function. 14 lines of code.

The Lesson

“When nothing makes sense, stop assuming the data is right. Literally print everything. Trust nothing. Be paranoid.”
